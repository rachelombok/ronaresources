NYU Student Resources

  • Virtual Computer Lab - The Virtual Computer Lab (VCL) provides NYU students with virtual access to academically relevant software applications. Includes software such as Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere Pro, AutoCAD, MATLab, Microsoft Project & Excel, and more.
  • Wasserman Services - Wasserman services and resources are still available for NYU undergraduate and graduates to use. Access university-wide jobs & internships, virual mock interviews, career/internship fairs and more through Handshake
  • VMock - An online resume analyzer that gives you instant targeted feedback on your CV or resume.
  • NYU Changemaker Fellowship - Fellowship for undergraduate students committed to addressing issues of economic and/or racial inequality through a summer internship experience. Reward: $5,0000. Deadline: April 10, 2020
  • LinkedIn Learning - LinkedIn Learning offers free resources and lessons for all NYU students on a variety of professional topics.
  • Kanbar Film & TV Support - The Kanbar Film & TV staff are here to help you through remote learning adjustments. Visit their site to learn about their resources!

Online Hackathons

  • Hack Quarantine 2020 - (March 23 - April 12, 2020) A fully-online, people-focused hackathon bringing people together to use their skills to help combat the issues the world is facing with the COVID-19 pandemic. Tracks include Tech & Health, Remote Working, Improving Awareness and Behavior, and Supporting People Quarantined or At Risk.
  • Google Code Jame - (April 3 - August 7, 2020)Google's annual Code Jam that challenges the online community to take on a series of challenging algorithmic puzzles designed by Google engineers. You’ll have a chance to earn the coveted title of Code Jam Champion and win $15,000 USD at the World Finals. Runs through the summer from April 3rd to August 7.
  • 2020 Skillsme Online Hackathon - (April 20 - Apil 27, 2020) This event is open to anyone who has a passion for coding and like challenges. During the Hackathon, you will build the project based on the topic which will be announced and published on Skillsme. All participants or teams will be judged the Skillsme Community by giving a ratings base on 6 criterias: Completeness, Functionality, Originality, Complexity, Uniqueness and Maintainable. At the end of the 7-day judging period, the one with the highest rating will win the $500 prize money. The winner will be announced on the 27th of April.

Online Learning

  • Codecademy - Offering 10,000 scholarships to Codecademy Pro for free to high school and college students across the world for the rest of the school year.
  • LinkedIn Learning - LinkedIn Learning offers a free 1-month trial for all of their courses on a variety of professional dev topics
  • Girls Who Code: Code at Home - In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, Girls Who Code is making CS educational activities available to download free of charge, to anyone who wants to access them. They will release activities weekly - some online, some offline, of varying levels of difficulty - over the next few months. Each activity will include a feature of a women in tech who pioneered innovative technology.
